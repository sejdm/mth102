  1. Show that the Binomial distribution with \(n=1\) becomes a Bernaulli distribution.

  2. Show that if \(X\) is a random variable with parameters \(n\) and \(p\) then \(E[X^k]= npE[(Y+1)^{k-1}]\) where \(Y\) is also a binomial random variable but with different paramaters. What are those paramaters?

  3. \(\frac{P\{X=k\}}{P\{X=k-1\}}=\frac{(n-k+1)p}{k(1-p)}\). Use this to find out which value of \(X\) has the highest probability. Show that before that the probabilities are increasing and after that they are decreasing.

  4. A coin is tossed 10 times. Use the above relation to find the probabilites of getting a head \(i\) times, for each \(i\).

  5. A coin with the probability of heads being \(p\), is tossed \(n\) times and the average number of heads is noted. Is this represented by binomial distribution? What is the expectation and variance?
