  1. Compute the moment generating function of the binomial distribution with parameters \(p\) and \(n\).

  2. Compute the moment generating fuction of the Poisson distribution with paramater \(\lambda\).

  3. Recall that if \(X\) and \(Y\) are independent then the moment generating function \(M_{X+Y}(t) = M_X(t) M_Y(t)\). Use this to derive the relations for \(E[X+Y]\) and \(\mathrm{Var}(X+Y)\).

  4. Derive a relation between \(M_{\frac{X-\mu}{\sigma}}(t)\) and \(M_X\).

  5. Supposing \(X_1, X_2, \ldots , X_n\) are independent and identical random variables, each with moment generating function \(M_t(X)\), mean 0, and variance 1. Let \(A_{n}\) denote that average of these random variables. Let \(\mu = E[A_n]\) and \(\sigma^2= \mathrm{Var}(A_{n})\) (note, these are the mean and variance of the average!).

    1. Compute \(M_{A_{n}}(t)\) in terms of \(M_X(t)\).

    2. What is the mean and variance of \(\frac{A_{n}-\mu}{\sigma}(t)\)?

    3. Compute \(M_{\frac{A_{n}-\mu}{\sigma}}(t)\) in terms of \(M_X(t)\).

    4. Show that \(\displaystyle\lim_{n\to \infty}M_{\frac{A_{n}-\mu}{\sigma}}(t) = e^{\frac{t^2}{2}}\). How does this prove the Central Limit Theorem for the special case where the identical distributions all had mean \(\mu\) and variance \(\sigma\)?
