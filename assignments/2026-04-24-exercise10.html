  1. If \(X\) is a binomial distribution wih parameters \(m\) and \(p\), and \(Y\) is a bimomial distribution with paramaters \(n\) and \(p\), then show that \(X+Y\) is binomial with parameter \(m+n\).

  2. Use the Central limit theorem to show that if \(X\) is a Poisson distribution with parameter \(\lambda\) which is large then \[P\{a < \frac{X - \lambda}{\sqrt{\lambda}} < b\} \approx \Phi(b) - \Phi(a)\]

  3. A die is rolled 1000 times. Use the Central Limit Theorem to find the approximate probability that the average number was more than 3. Leave the answer in terms of \(\Phi\).

  4. Use the Central Limit Theorem to show that for any positive real number \(\epsilon\), however small, \[\displaystyle\lim_{n\to \infty}P\{-\epsilon < \frac{X_1 + \cdots + X_n}{n} - \mu < \epsilon\} = 1\]
